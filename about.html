About

Ecophysiology across the fern life cycle.

Daniela Aros-Mualin is a postdoctoral researcher in Scott McAdam's lab in the Department of Botany and Plant Pathology at Purdue University. She studies how ferns and lycophytes regulate water and carbon across environments, with particular attention to stomatal control, gas exchange, and the ecological meaning of physiological traits.

Her research connects field ecology, comparative physiology, and macroevolutionary questions. By working across sporophytes, gametophytes, communities, and global datasets, she asks how ancient vascular plant lineages persist in modern environments and what they reveal about the evolution of plant function.

Background

Training and current work

Daniela completed an MSc at the University of Bonn in Germany, then pursued her PhD with Michael Kessler at the University of Zurich. Her doctoral work focused on water-carbon relations in ferns and built the foundation for her current research on fern physiology, environmental stress, and evolutionary ecology.

At Purdue University, she works as a postdoc in the McAdam lab, where her research continues to examine how fern stomata, light responses, and water-use strategies differ from those of seed plants.
