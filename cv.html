Daniela Aros-Mualin

Postdoc, McAdam lab, Department of Botany and Plant Pathology, Purdue University.

Summary

Research profile

Daniela Aros-Mualin is an ecophysiologist studying fern and lycophyte water-carbon relations, stomatal function, desiccation tolerance, and the ecological significance of independent plant generations.

Current position

Postdoc, McAdam lab, Department of Botany and Plant Pathology, Purdue University

Education path

MSc, University of Bonn, Germany · PhD, Michael Kessler lab, University of Zurich · Postdoc, Purdue University

Key affiliations

Purdue University · University of Zurich · University of Bonn

Career highlights

  • Published comparative fern and lycophyte physiology work in Plant Physiology, Annals of Botany, Frontiers in Plant Science, and Journal of Biogeography.
  • Completed the dissertation Water-Carbon Relations in Ferns at the University of Zurich.
  • Developed a research program linking field ecology, gas exchange, trait diversity, and macroevolutionary patterns in seedless vascular plants.

Research interests

  • Fern and lycophyte ecophysiology
  • Stomatal regulation and gas exchange
  • Water relations, poikilohydry, and desiccation tolerance
  • Functional diversity and biogeography
  • Gametophyte and sporophyte ecology
